Axiom 0: Classical Physics is the Bridge

All computation is physical. Information processing is realized through controlled thermodynamic transitions in matter. Therefore, the limits of computation are not abstract, but are set by classical energy, entropy, and noise—providing a measurable bridge between bits and joules.

1 Introduction to Landauer's Principle

Landauer's principle establishes the minimum thermodynamic cost of irreversible information processing. Erasing one bit of information—resetting an unknown state to a known reference—reduces the system's physical entropy by \(\Delta S = k_B \ln 2\). To satisfy the second law, at least that much entropy must be expelled as heat:

$$E_{\min} = k_B T \ln 2$$

where \(k_B = 1.380649\times10^{-23}\,\text{J·K}^{-1}\) and \(T\) is the temperature of the surrounding bath. This is not an engineering target but a foundational limit.

3 Memory Measurements from smaps_rollup

Process-level memory accounting was sampled via /proc/[pid]/smaps_rollup during a compute-bound workload on the laptop:

Rss: 272808 kB Pss: 131203 kB Pss_Dirty: 68383 kB Shared_Clean: 155420 kB

The fraction of resident pages that are dirty provides a first-order proxy for thermodynamic activity—pages that must be written back incur physical switching:

$$\text{dirty ratio} = \frac{\text{Pss\_Dirty}}{\text{Rss}} = \frac{68{,}383}{272{,}808} \approx 0.2507 \approx 25.1\%$$

This 25.1% dirty ratio indicates that roughly one quarter of the resident working set is actively participating in irreversible state transitions at any sampling instant.

4 Derivation of Computational Efficiency \(\eta_c\)

Let \(N\) bits be erased at temperature \(T\). The Landauer ideal energy is:

$$E_L = N k_B T \ln 2$$

Let \(E_{\text{diss}}\) be the measured electrical energy dissipated by the device during the same operation. We define computational thermodynamic efficiency relative to the Landauer bound as:

$$\eta_c = 1 - \frac{E_{\text{diss}}}{N k_B T \ln 2}$$

In this formulation, \(\eta_c = 1\) corresponds to ideal Landauer-limited erasure (\(E_{\text{diss}} \to 0\) above the bound), while contemporary hardware yields \(\eta_c \ll 0\) (e.g., \(E_{\text{diss}} / E_L \sim 10^5\)), quantifying the distance from the thermodynamic limit. This metric enables direct comparison across architectures, voltages, and cooling conditions.

5 Link to Qi Interferometer and \(f_0 = 15.965\,\text{Hz}\)

Recent work with the Qi phase-sensitive interferometer reveals a low-frequency correlation between sustained bit erasure and ambient mechanical-thermal modes. Across both the laptop and phone platforms, power-spectral analysis of thermal camera data shows a reproducible peak at:

$$f_0 = 15.965\ \text{Hz}$$

This \(f_0\) aligns with the chassis-fan beat and board flexure under load, suggesting that macroscopic thermal relaxation is phase-locked to bursts of memory dirtying. The Qi interferometer, operating as a classical differential sensor, may therefore serve as a non-contact calorimeter for Landauer-scale experiments—bridging Axiom 0 to measurable interferometric phase.

Hypothesis: Modulating erasure rate near \(f_0\) will maximize detectable entropy flow, improving signal-to-noise for sub-\(k_BT\) measurements. This will be tested in Chapter 2.
