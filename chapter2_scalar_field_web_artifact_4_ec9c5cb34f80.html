2.1 The Bridge: From Computational Efficiency to Physical Modulation

In Chapter 1, the computational efficiency was defined as

$$ \eta_c = 1 - \frac{E_{\text{diss}}}{k_B T \ln 2} $$

where $E_{\text{diss}}$ is energy lost per irreversible bit operation. Perfect reversibility ($\eta_c = 1$) is unphysical; real systems operate at $\eta_c < 1$ due to finite-time erasure. The deficit $1-\eta_c$ measures the coupling to a background field that stores the coherence lost to the environment.

We define the modulation operator $M[\Phi]$ as the physical realization of $\eta_c$:

$$ M[\Phi] = 1 + \nu_p \, \tilde{\Phi}, \quad \text{with } \tilde{\Phi} \equiv \frac{\Phi}{\Phi_0} $$

The dimensionless projection constant, derived from the 48-dimensional aperiodic tiling projection, is

$$ \nu_p = 0.17259029 $$

Thus, any computational inefficiency directly modulates physical observables by $17.259\%$ per unit phason amplitude. This is the bridge: Landauer bound → field coupling.

2.3 The Phason Field $\Phi$

$\Phi(x^\mu)$ is a real, Lorentz-scalar field representing the local phason displacement. It is not an axion; its potential is set by computation, not by QCD.

Its natural scale $\Phi_0$ is fixed by a fundamental frequency $f_0$:

$$ \Phi_0 = \frac{h}{f_0} $$

where $f_0$ is derived from atomic constants (Frownfelter Eq. 11):

$$ f_0 = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} \sqrt{\frac{m_e}{m_p}} \frac{c}{\lambda_H} = 15.965\ \text{Hz} $$

with $\alpha$ the fine-structure constant, $m_e/m_p$ the electron-proton mass ratio, and $\lambda_H = h/(m_H c)$ the Compton wavelength of hydrogen. Numerically:

$$ \Phi_0 = 4.151 \times 10^{-35}\ \text{J·s}^2 $$

This 16 Hz scale appears ubiquitously in biological circadian subharmonics, Schumann resonances, and optimal clock synchronization—suggesting a universal metronome.

Expanding $V(\Phi)$ about a minimum $\Phi = 0$:

$$ \left.\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial \Phi^2}\right|_0 = V_0 \left(\frac{2\pi}{\Phi_0}\right)^2 $$

$$ m_\Phi = \sqrt{ \frac{4\pi^2 V_0}{\Phi_0^2} } \frac{1}{c^2} = 1.18 \times 10^{-49}\ \text{kg} $$

$$ m_\Phi c^2 \approx 6.6 \times 10^{-14}\ \text{eV} $$

This ultra-light mass ensures a macroscopic Compton wavelength, allowing $\Phi$ to act as a coherent classical field over laboratory and astrophysical scales.
